Jonathan LeVine Gallery is pleased to announce he will now accept Dash (Digital Cash), a cryptocurrency that allows for instantaneous transactions.

Jonathan LeVine has always had a dedication to the avant­garde, bringing to the forefront underground art with a unique counter­culture point of view. In 1994, he began independently curating exhibitions at punk and alternative rock venues in New Jersey and New York. In 2001, LeVine opened his own gallery called Tin Man Alley in New Hope, Pennsylvania. Four years later he renamed and moved the gallery to the epicenter of the contemporary art world, Manhattan’s Chelsea district, and became one of the first gallerists to sell fine art online.

Since 2005, Jonathan LeVine Gallery has been an essential venue for Street Art, ephemeral work placed in public urban environments, and Pop Surrealism, work influenced by illustration, comic book art and pop culture imagery. Over the last ten years the gallery has worked with over 300 artists and remains true to its mission of showing an alternative viewpoint within the New York art market.

Node40 assisted Jonathan LeVine Gallery by setting up the infrastructure necessary to accept Dash, allowing them to effortlessly send and receive money anywhere in the world. Using a system comparable to online banking, Dash is stored in a digital wallet and transferred directly from person to person without a middleman, meaning there is little to no fee to transfer funds, funds cannot be frozen and there are no prerequisites or arbitrary limits.

Dash is a privacy­ centric structure. Transactions are visible only to you and the other party involved, giving Jonathan LeVine Gallery clients the opportunity to remain anonymous while making payments, similar to using cash. Payments are received almost immediately and advanced encryption ensures complete security. Dash can also be easily converted into another cryptocurrencies, such as Bitcoin.

For more information about Dash and to setup your digital wallet, please visit:
www.dashpay.io
